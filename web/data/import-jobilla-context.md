
# The Jobilla Primer: Core Function and Goals

This document provides a clear, concise overview of Jobilla's business model, objectives, digital marketing campaigns rules, frameworks and brand voice.

## What Jobilla Does

Jobilla is a recruitment technology and marketing company. Its primary function is to help other businesses (its clients) find and hire qualified job candidates.

The core of its method is a modern digital marketing funnel, which works in three main steps:

1.  **Advertisement**: Jobilla creates and places targeted ads on social media platforms (like Facebook and Instagram) to reach potential candidates. This includes people who are not actively looking for a new job ("passive candidates").
2.  **Landing Page**: When a person clicks the ad, they are taken to a custom landing page. This page is designed to be engaging and quickly explain the benefits of the job and the company.
3.  **Questionnaire**: Instead of asking for a CV, the landing page invites the candidate to take a short, 3-5 minute questionnaire. This form asks questions relevant to the job to quickly assess if there is a mutual fit.

In short, Jobilla runs end-to-end digital recruitment campaigns on behalf of its clients, managing everything from content creation to candidate qualification.

## What Jobilla's Goals Are

Jobilla's objectives can be understood from three perspectives: for its clients, for the candidates, and for the market itself.

* **For Clients**: The main goal is to solve hiring challenges efficiently. This means reducing the time it takes to hire someone, improving the quality of candidates, and finding talent that traditional methods (like job boards) would miss.
* **For Candidates**: A key goal is to create a better, more respectful application experience. By removing the need for a CV upfront and using a quick, relevant questionnaire, Jobilla aims to make the process faster, more engaging, and less biased for job seekers. This is their "candidate-centric" philosophy.
* **For the Recruitment Market**: Jobilla's ultimate goal is to disrupt outdated recruitment practices. They aim to prove that a proactive, marketing-based approach is more effective than the passive "post and pray" model of traditional job boards, especially in winning the modern "war for talent."

## The Jobilla Funnel: A Precise Description

The Jobilla funnel is a three-stage digital recruitment process designed to guide a potential candidate from initial awareness to qualification.

* **The Ad (Attention)**: The process begins with a targeted social media ad (on platforms like Facebook and Instagram) that captures the attention of both active and passive candidates.
* **The Landing Page (Persuasion)**: When a candidate clicks the ad, they are directed to a landing page. This page informs them about the role's benefits and the company culture, convincing them to take the next step.
* **The Questionnaire (Qualification)**: Instead of a traditional CV submission, the final stage is a short, 3-5 minute questionnaire. Its purpose is to efficiently qualify the candidate by assessing if there is a mutual fit for the position.

# Campaign Creation Guide

This guide explains how to build Jobilla Digital Headhunting (DH) campaigns. It is based on the Jobilla concept and uses information from provided internal documents. Follow these steps to create effective campaigns.

## 1. Introduction: The Jobilla DH Concept

### What Makes Jobilla Campaigns Different?

Jobilla campaigns focus on making it easy for candidates to apply and efficient for recruiters to process applications.

**Easier for Candidates:**
* We target ads only to people likely interested.
* We talk about opportunities (what you get), not requirements.
* Applying is easy with a few simple questions.
* Candidates get immediate feedback on their suitability (A, B, C score).

**More Efficient for Recruiters:**
* Ads target professionals in the field.
* The questionnaire focuses on required skills, education, and experience.
* Scoring makes it easy to see who to contact first.

### The Candidate-Driven Approach

This means we communicate differently with potential candidates.
* **Talk like a friend**: The company and candidate are on the same level.
* **Highlight benefits**: Focus on what the candidate gets if they switch jobs. Ask "Why are we better than others?" Mention non-material benefits (like work pace).
* **Never talk about requirements** on the landing page or ads. Key skills are checked in the questionnaire.
* **No promise of a job**, but no rejection if skills aren't perfect (unless a specific skill is absolutely mandatory).
* **Use images** that attract and inspire potential candidates.
* **Keep it short and easy**: The landing page and questionnaire should be quick to complete.
* **Target passive candidates**: Make applying feel effortless.

### Overview of the Campaign Creation Workflow

1.  Gather necessary information (job details, criteria, company info, assets).
2.  Create Visual Ads.
3.  Design the Landing Page.
4.  Build the Questionnaire (including Attributes and Scoring).
5.  Set up Result and Thank You pages.
6.  Launch and monitor the campaign.

## 4. Questionnaire Construction

### How to Create an Effective Questionnaire

The questionnaire gathers key information and helps score candidates.

#### Recommended Structure

A typical structure includes questions about:
* (Optional: Character/candidate-driven opening question)
* Years of work experience
* Specific job duties / skills
* Education
* Language skills
* Required cards/licenses/special training (e.g., driving license, VCA)
* Specialist skills ("unicorn" skills)
* (Optional: Candidate-driven closing question, e.g., "What do you value?")

#### Using Attributes & Scoring Effectively

Attributes are used to automatically grade candidates based on their answers.

* **What are Attributes?** They represent key criteria for the job (e.g., "Experience Level", "Specific Skill", "Required License"). The system uses answers linked to attributes to calculate a score.
* **Attribute Types**:
    * **Scaled**: Used when there are levels of suitability (e.g., experience years: Excellent, Good, Average, Poor). Usually, 3 levels are enough.
    * **Boolean**: Used for Yes/No criteria (e.g., "Has Driving License?").
* **Scoring Logic Explained**:
    * **A**: Candidate meets all essential requirements.
    * **B**: Candidate is almost suitable (missing 1 or more required attributes).
    * **C**: Candidate is unsuitable (meets none of the 'A' criteria OR fails a "Disqualified if not met" requirement).
    * **A+**: Candidate meets all 'A' criteria AND has a specific desired special skill.
* **Disqualification Rules**: You can mark an attribute answer as "Disqualified if not met". If a candidate chooses this answer, they automatically become 'C', regardless of other answers. Use this for absolute must-haves (e.g., required degree/license).
* **Setting up A+ Attributes**: In the attribute settings, you can define a level that counts towards an A+ score (e.g., "Ideally to be at least Yes"). Link this to the relevant answer option in the question.
* **Creating Attributes in the System**:
    * Attributes are created under "Job Details" in the Jobilla platform.
    * Use the correct category: Education & Licenses, Skills & Experience, Personality, Availability.
    * Click "+ Add an attribute". Choose Boolean or Scaled. Name it clearly. Define options (for Scaled).
* **Important**: Do not rename attributes used in previous campaigns. Create new ones if needed.
* **Tip**: Keep the attribute setup as simple as possible. Complexity often makes it harder for the recruiter. Only create attributes for genuine needs, usually for hard facts like experience, education, specific essential skills/tasks. Avoid rating character questions unless directly critical for the job.

#### Question Style & Content

* Keep questions clear, simple, and quick to answer.
* Use multiple-choice questions where possible.
* Ensure answer options cover all likely scenarios, including "No experience" or "Does not apply".
* For multi-select questions where having no experience is important, create a specific "No Experience" Boolean attribute and link it to the "I don't have experience" answer option.

#### Standard Contact Questions

Always include standard questions at the end to collect contact details:
* Name (First and Last)
* Email Address
* Phone Number
* Consent question (asking permission to contact them about the role)

#### Handling Multiple Languages/Regions

* Use the provided standard questions for contact info in the target language.
* Follow the translation process guidelines if translating a full campaign.

#### Result & Thank You Page Content

Set up the pages shown after the candidate submits the questionnaire.
* **Result Page**: Shows the candidate their score (A+, A, B, C, or "?") and a corresponding message. This is where contact details are collected.
* **Thank You Page**: Confirms submission and may include a link to the company website.

#### General Advice

* Focus attributes on hard facts (experience, education, essential skills).
* Don't make the scoring too complex.
* Ensure questions and answers directly relate to the job's key criteria.

### Questionnaire Checklist

- [ ] Does the questionnaire follow the recommended structure?
- [ ] Are attributes created for key requirements?
- [ ] Is the scoring logic (A/B/C/A+) set up correctly?
- [ ] Are disqualification rules used appropriately for must-haves?

### What is Candidate Driven?

In recruitment, "candidate-driven" refers to a job market condition where job seekers have more power and influence in the hiring process due to high demand for talent and limited supply of qualified candidates.

This means:
* Candidates can be selective about job opportunities
* Employers must compete to attract and retain talent
* Job seekers often receive multiple offers
* Companies need to offer better compensation and benefits
* The hiring process tends to focus more on candidate experience

Essentially itâ€™s about: **All information immediately vs. fragmenting data collection.**

Real candidate-driven way of sharing information & asking questions focus on what candidates genuinely need to know:
* Actual day-to-day responsibilities
* Clear growth opportunities and learning paths
* Team dynamics and management style
* Concrete examples of projects/challenges
* Flexibility and autonomy levels

Soft, low-value information & questions:
* Often waste candidates' time
* Sometimes create false expectations
* Don't help candidates make informed decisions
* Provide no actionable data for either party

### Better definition of "candidate-driven":

* Information & questions that help candidates evaluate fit realistically, with low effort
* Information that enables informed career decisions
* Content that respects candidates' time and intelligence
* Data points that both parties can act upon
* Questions that reveal mutual expectations

Being candidate-driven doesn't mean being just soft or vague, it means helping candidates make informed and low-barriered decisions while gathering meaningful data for hiring decisions â€“> **Little decisions step by step when both parties get more information!**

### Core principles for truly candidate-driven questions:

* **Reciprocal value**
    * Questions should help both parties assess fit
    * Answers should be actionable for both candidate and recruiter
    * Information gathered should directly relate to job success
* **Respect for time**
    * Each question should have a clear purpose
    * Answers should help candidates self-select in/out
    * Questions should be specific enough to prevent generic responses
* **Reality-based assessment**
    * Focus on real scenarios instead of hypotheticals
    * Ask about actual experiences rather than theoretical situations
    * Include context about real challenges they'll face
* **Future-focused development**
    * Questions about growth should be specific to your company's opportunities
    * Discuss concrete learning paths rather than vague aspirations
    * Connect candidate's goals with actual company initiatives

* Doesn't relate to actual job requirements
* Provides no useful information for decision-making

### General Usages

**Allowed:**
* Use a greyscale monochromous logo.
* Use a dark purple version of the logo.
* Use the font without the Jobibot.
* Use the Jobibot without the font.

**Not Allowed:**
* Do not add any effect to the logo.
* Do not change the color of any part of the polychromous logo.
* Do not tilt the logo.
* Do not change the font of the logo.
* Do not distort the logo.
* Do not use an outline around the logo.
* Do not change the size between the elements of the logo.
* Do not dismantle the Jobibot or move its elements.

### Usages on a Background

* Our logo can be used on a plain background color from our palette at a maximum tint of 18%.
* On an image, the monochromous logo should be used if and only if the background allows sufficient contrast. The background image should be simple enough not to create confusion and create enough visibility.
* Always ensure enough contrast between the logo and the background.
* Do not use any of the logos on a background that does not bring sufficient contrast.
* Do not use any other background colors than our palette at a maximum tint of 18%.
* The heptagon can be used as a colored background element for our Jobibot.
